What is The Odyssey  ?

Τί ἐστιν τοῦτο;
So he spoke, and they were all hushed in silence, and were held spell-bound throughout the shadowy halls.
ὣς ἔφαθ᾽, οἱ δ᾽ ἄρα πάντες ἀκὴν ἐγένοντο σιωπῇ,
κηληθμῷ δ᾽ ἔσχοντο κατὰ μέγαρα σκιόεντα.


Book XI, 333-334

Ever since Homer wrote The Odyssey, it has been on the lips of bards and the minds of men. Homer laid the foundations of literature by speaking to the depths of the human condition.

Who hasn't felt like a king deposed, adrift, wanting to go home? Who hasn't struggled with capricious gods, treacherous companions, or the harsh necessity of nature? The Odyssey tells a sobering and familiar tale: no matter how victorious we may have been in our battles, our trails are not yet over.

Take courage, my heart: you have been through worse than this.
τέτλαθι δή, κραδίη: καὶ κύντερον ἄλλο ποτ᾽ ἔτλης.


Book XX, 18

Throughout the centuries The Odyssey has inspired countless translations and commentaries. Scholarship abounds for this poem. While the interface may be robust, the aesthetic is often lacking:

So true is it that the gods do not give gracious gifts to all alike,
not form nor mind nor eloquence.
For one man is inferior in comeliness...
οὕτως οὐ πάντεσσι θεοὶ χαρίεντα διδοῦσιν
ἀνδράσιν, οὔτε φυὴν οὔτ’ ἂρ φρένας οὔτ’ ἀγορητύν.
ἄλλος μὲν γάρ τ᾽ εἶδος ἀκιδνότερος πέλει ἀνήρ


Book VIII, 167-168

This project Odyssey.io is an attempt to bring together the wealth of resources dedicated to The Odyssey with a simple, pleasant, and interactive design. Here you can navigate through the books of Homer's classic text. Toggle individual verses or whole books between Greek and English. This site is idea for anyone looking for an elegant way to read The Odyssey while also gaining familiarity with Homer's lexicon. If that's you, let's get started...

Common Questions
Συχνές Ερωτήσεις
  • What translation are you using?
    Currently I am using the A. T. Murray translation of The Odyssey (1919). Murray's translation has a lot going for it: it's public domain, its syntax roughly matches the Greek verses, it doesn't use 'Ulysses,' and it has strong scholarly support. Originally published by Harvard for their Loeb Classics Series, it's also used by The Perseus Project.
  • How many books are done right now?
    Book I is a quarter done and Book II is started. Murray's translation is prose, so I have to manually put the verse breaks in the English. Now that the site is roughly built out, I can spend more time on versification.
  • Are you working on any other works right now?
    The plan is to work like Western literature: get Homer right, and then go from there. There are a few other books I have in mind though.
  • What do you do? Are you a teacher?
    For two years I taught a classical schools here in Michigan. My Greek came from studying theology and being Eastern Orthodox. As a freelancer, I've edited a few textbooks in Biblical Greek. But most of the time, I do illustration, design, and web development work.
  • Where can I find more of your work?
    You can see some of my web work on GitHub and some of my photography and drawings on Instagram. If you need anything in those areas, feel free to Email Me. I'm always looking for new work.
